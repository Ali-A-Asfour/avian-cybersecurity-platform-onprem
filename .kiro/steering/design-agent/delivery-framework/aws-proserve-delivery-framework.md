# AWS ProServe Delivery Framework - Implementation Guidance

## Overview

This steering document establishes the foundational framework for delivering quality implementation plans based on AWS Professional Services' proven methodologies. Our delivery approach follows the AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF) and Day 1 Delivery (D1D) principles to ensure consistent, bar-raising customer experiences.

## Core Delivery Philosophy

### What is Delivery?
Delivery is what we sell and provide as Professional Services. We follow the AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF), a shared mental model for cloud transformation that leverages AWS experience and best practices to enable customers to:
- Identify and prioritize transformation opportunities
- Evaluate and improve cloud readiness  
- Iteratively evolve transformation roadmaps

### Key Customer Business Outcomes (CBOs)
All implementations must target these core customer business outcomes:
- **Reduced Business Risk**: Enhanced security, compliance, and operational resilience
- **Improved ESG Performance**: Environmental, social, and governance improvements
- **Increased Revenue**: New revenue streams and market opportunities
- **Operational Efficiency**: Cost optimization and process improvements

### Day 1 Delivery (D1D) Mindset
We maintain a Day 1 mindset that is:
- **Curious**: Always learning and questioning assumptions
- **Inventive**: Creative problem-solving and innovation
- **Scrappy**: Resourceful and efficient execution
- **Fast**: Rapid delivery without compromising quality

## Global Delivery Framework (GDF) Components

### 1. Worldwide (WW) Standards
The delivery bar has been defined through WW Standards that ensure consistent, high-quality delivery across all engagements.

### 2. Worldwide (WW) Guidance
Each standard has supporting guidance that helps teams meet or exceed the standard. This serves as leading practice and suggested implementation methodology.

### 3. ProServe Delivery Practices (PDP)
- Single, searchable destination for delivery best practices
- Each GDF process has playbooks, runbooks, practice considerations, and FAQs
- Adheres to ProServe tenet: "Build Once, Use Often, Improve Always"

## Implementation Quality Requirements

### Security-First Approach
Regardless of what we sell, how we deliver should prioritize security:
- Pre-Engagement Security Assessment (PESA) required for all SOWs
- Security considerations integrated throughout delivery lifecycle
- Proper resource allocation for security controls
- Contract considerations for secure engagement

### Customer Business Outcomes Focus
Every implementation must:
- Define measurable customer business outcomes
- Articulate impact of deliverables
- Utilize CBO playbook guidance
- Position AWS as trusted advisor
- Enhance credibility through outcome delivery

### Consistent Experience Standards
- Bar-raising delivery quality
- Consistent customer experience across all touchpoints
- Proactive risk management and mitigation
- Clear communication and stakeholder alignment
- Continuous improvement and learning

## Quality Assurance Mechanisms

### Opportunity Quality Review (OQR)
- Completed during technical validation stage
- Strengthens business case in proposals
- Inspects likelihood of successful delivery
- Supports sales and delivery team efforts

### Delivery Quality Inspection (DQI)
- Self-inspection for long-running teams
- Sanity check delivery governance processes
- Beneficial for teams 120-365 days after project start
- Expedites back-to-green planning for Red/Amber engagements

### Metrics and Reporting
- Panorama dashboard for comprehensive reporting
- Adoption tracking as ProServe annual goal
- Transparency and ownership principles
- Regular business reviews and goal tracking

## Success Metrics

### Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)
- Critical feedback mechanism aligned with Customer Obsession
- Survey sent at end of all engagements where legally possible
- Measures satisfaction with products and services

### Deliverable Acceptance
- Establish tracking documents at kickoff
- Monitor and update progress throughout project
- Validate customer expectations are met
- Ensure compliance with contractual obligations

### Continuous Improvement
- Document lessons learned and best practices
- Share success stories across organization
- Refine processes and methodologies
- Foster culture of continuous improvement

## Implementation Phases

### 1. Sales to Delivery (S2D) Phase
- Align #OneTeam on scope and goals
- Knowledge and artifact transfer
- Stakeholder introductions
- Seamless customer experience

### 2. Launch Phase
- Sprint 0 preparation and execution
- Customer kickoff and team onboarding
- Establish governance and communication
- Set shared expectations

### 3. Manage & Inspect Phase
- Track progress toward milestones
- Ensure quality day-to-day management
- Strengthen AWS-customer relationship
- Continuous inspection and adaptation

### 4. Close & Delivery to Sales (D2S) Phase
- Finalize contractual obligations
- Communicate results and obtain approval
- Knowledge transfer and documentation
- Retrospective for continuous improvement

- Decrease concessions and risks

### Team Benefits

- Fewer concessions and issues

### Customer Benefits

## Implementation Guidelines

1. **Follow GDF Structure**: Use globally adopted delivery process
2. **Maintain #OneTeam Approach**: Ensure all stakeholders participate
3. **Prioritize Security**: Integrate security throughout delivery lifecycle
4. **Focus on CBOs**: Align all activities with customer business outcomes
5. **Use Standard Tools**: Leverage PDP content catalog and Panorama reporting
6. **Continuous Improvement**: Apply lessons learned and best practices
7. **Quality Gates**: Implement OQR and DQI processes
8. **Stakeholder Engagement**: Maintain regular communication and alignment

This framework ensures we deliver consistent, high-quality implementations that exceed customer expectations while maintaining AWS's reputation for excellence and innovation.
