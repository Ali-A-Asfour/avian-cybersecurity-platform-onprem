# Global Delivery Framework (GDF) Implementation Guide

## Overview

The Global Delivery Framework (GDF) is the globally adopted delivery process within AWS Professional Services. It combines proven Amazonian ways of working with leading industry best practices, providing all tools and resources needed to launch, manage, inspect, and close engagements consistently across all regions and practices.

## Why GDF Matters

### Business Impact
- **Consistent Ways of Working**: Increase collaboration, improve project outcomes, reduce miscommunications, and eliminate unnecessary overhead
- **Quality Foundations**: Reduce customer confusion in CSAT responses and decrease concessions
- **Team Efficiency**: Faster onboarding and offboarding, fewer concessions, reduced risks becoming issues
- **Continuous Learning**: Opportunity to keep learning, being curious, and adopting ways of working

### Customer Benefits
- Consistent, bar-raising experience across all engagements
- Security prioritized throughout delivery lifecycle
- Predictable quality and delivery standards
- Reduced confusion and improved satisfaction

## GDF Components

### 1. Worldwide (WW) Standards
Global structure for delivering customer business outcomes while maintaining flexibility for customer needs.

#### Core Standards Categories
- **Project Management**: Standardized PM practices and governance
- **Quality Assurance**: Consistent quality gates and inspection processes
- **Security**: Mandatory security controls and compliance requirements
- **Communication**: Standardized stakeholder engagement and reporting
- **Documentation**: Consistent deliverable formats and content standards
- **Knowledge Transfer**: Standardized handoff and transition processes

#### Standards Implementation
- [ ] **Adoption Tracking**: Monitor GDF adoption as ProServe annual goal
- [ ] **Compliance Measurement**: Regular assessment of standards adherence
- [ ] **Quality Metrics**: Track delivery quality and customer satisfaction
- [ ] **Continuous Improvement**: Regular updates based on field experience

### 2. Worldwide (WW) Guidance
Supporting guidance for each standard that serves as leading practice and suggested implementation methodology.

#### Guidance Categories
- **Process Guidance**: Step-by-step implementation instructions
- **Best Practices**: Proven approaches and methodologies
- **Templates and Tools**: Standardized formats and resources
- **Examples and Case Studies**: Real-world implementation examples
- **Troubleshooting**: Common issues and resolution approaches

#### Practice Considerations
- Defined at practice level and approved by global delivery steering committee
- Documented centrally and inspected regularly
- Ensure standards are being met while accommodating regional needs
- Regular review and update based on practice feedback

### 3. ProServe Delivery Practices (PDP) Content Catalog
Single, searchable destination for delivery best practices and everything needed for successful delivery.

#### Content Structure
- **Playbooks**: Comprehensive guides for specific delivery scenarios
- **Runbooks**: Step-by-step operational procedures
- **Practice Considerations**: Regional and practice-specific adaptations
- **FAQs**: Frequently asked questions and answers
- **Quick Links**: Critical guidance accessible through Salesforce (SFDC)

#### Content Management
- **Build Once, Use Often, Improve Always**: Core tenet for content development
- **Shared Knowledge Base**: Centralized repository for all delivery knowledge
- **Regular Updates**: Continuous improvement based on field experience
- **Search Functionality**: Easy discovery of relevant content and guidance

## GDF Implementation Phases

### Phase 1: Engagement Setup and Planning

#### Sprint 0 Implementation
**Objective**: Complete foundational work before customer engagement begins

**Key Activities (2 weeks prior to customer kickoff):**
- [ ] **Team Onboarding**: Ensure all team members understand GDF processes
- [ ] **Governance Setup**: Establish project management structures per GDF standards
- [ ] **Tooling Configuration**: Set up required tools and access per WW guidance
- [ ] **Process Alignment**: Align team on GDF processes and practice considerations
- [ ] **Quality Planning**: Define quality gates and inspection schedules

**Deliverables:**
- Team onboarding completion confirmation
- Governance structure documentation
- Tool configuration and access validation
- Process alignment and training records
- Quality plan and inspection schedule

#### #OneTeam Meeting Setup
**Objective**: Establish collaborative platform for all stakeholders

**Required Participants:**
- AWS Sales Team (AM, TAM, SA, CSM)
- ProServe Sales Team
- Delivery Team
- Internal Partners (if applicable)

**Meeting Objectives:**
- Create unified platform for early and frequent collaboration
- Align expectations, resources, and strategies
- Identify and address potential roadblocks
- Support flawless delivery exceeding customer expectations

### Phase 2: Launch and Customer Kickoff

#### Customer Kickoff Standards
**Objective**: Set positive tone and accelerate progress

**Required Elements:**
- [ ] **Project Charter**: Clear scope, objectives, and success criteria
- [ ] **Team Introductions**: Roles, responsibilities, and contact information
- [ ] **Communication Plan**: Meeting cadences, reporting, escalation paths
- [ ] **Timeline Review**: Milestones, dependencies, critical path
- [ ] **Success Metrics**: Measurable outcomes and acceptance criteria
- [ ] **Risk Assessment**: Known risks and mitigation strategies

**Success Criteria:**
- Customer confidence in delivery team established
- Clear understanding of project scope and requirements
- Communication channels and governance established
- Risk mitigation strategies agreed upon
- Positive momentum and team alignment achieved

### Phase 3: Manage and Inspect

#### Agile Delivery Practices
**Daily Operations:**
- **Stand-ups**: Progress updates, impediment identification, coordination
- **Backlog Management**: Regular refinement and prioritization
- **Sprint Planning**: Capacity planning and commitment setting
- **Stakeholder Syncs**: Regular alignment and feedback collection

#### Quality Inspection Framework
**Delivery Quality Inspection (DQI):**
- **Timing**: 120-365 days after project start, new team members, new phases
- **Scope**: Sanity check delivery governance processes
- **Participants**: Engagement leadership team (EM, delivery leads, CPM/ESM, CDAs)
- **Outcome**: Back-to-green plan for Red/Amber engagements

**Inspection Areas:**
- [ ] **Governance Effectiveness**: Project management and decision-making
- [ ] **Communication Quality**: Stakeholder engagement and transparency
- [ ] **Risk Management**: Identification, assessment, and mitigation
- [ ] **Quality Standards**: Adherence to GDF standards and guidance
- [ ] **Customer Satisfaction**: Feedback and relationship health

#### Continuous Improvement
**Retrospective Process:**
- Regular team retrospectives for process improvement
- Lesson capture and sharing across organization
- Best practice identification and documentation
- Process refinement based on field experience

### Phase 4: Close and Transition

#### Deliverable Acceptance
**Objective**: Ensure customer expectations are met and contractual obligations fulfilled

**Key Activities:**
- [ ] **Quality Validation**: Comprehensive review of all deliverables
- [ ] **Customer Approval**: Formal acceptance and sign-off process
- [ ] **Documentation**: Complete artifact repository and knowledge transfer
- [ ] **Compliance Verification**: Ensure all contractual obligations met

#### Knowledge Transfer Processes
**Delivery to Sales (D2S):**
- Realign #OneTeam post-engagement
- Transition knowledge to internal stakeholders
- Conclude with retrospective for future improvement
- Capture potential future opportunities

**Delivery to Operations (D2O):**
- Knowledge sharing between Professional Services and Enterprise Support
- Ensure continuity of safe and effective operations
- Document solution weaknesses and risks not solved
- Committed TAM participation in knowledge transfer

## Metrics and Reporting

### Panorama Dashboard Implementation
**Purpose**: One-stop-shop for all reporting needs with accountability, ownership, and visibility

#### Dashboard Components
- **Adoption Metrics**: GDF adoption tracking across practices
- **Quality Metrics**: Delivery quality and customer satisfaction
- **Performance Metrics**: On-time, on-budget delivery rates
- **Risk Metrics**: Risk identification and mitigation effectiveness
- **Trend Analysis**: Performance trends and improvement opportunities

#### Stakeholder Usage
| Role | Dashboard Usage | Ownership |
|------|----------------|-----------|
| **ProServe Sales** | Monitor sales/customer metrics, provide feedback | Use dashboard for business insights |
| **ProServe Delivery** | Track project-level metrics and quality | Utilize for project management |
| **Shared Delivery Leaders** | Review area and practice metrics | Business reviews and coaching |
| **Delivery Managers** | Monitor delivery metrics and quality | Team enablement and coaching |
| **Strategy & Operations** | Analysis and custom reporting | Dashboard development and maintenance |
| **D1D Team** | Dashboard development and improvement | Continuous enhancement |

### Success Metrics
- **GDF Adoption Rate**: Percentage of engagements following GDF processes
- **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)**: Post-engagement customer feedback scores
- **Delivery Performance**: On-time, on-budget delivery achievement
- **Quality Standards**: Adherence to WW standards and guidance
- **Risk Management**: Successful mitigation of identified risks

## Quality Assurance and Governance

### Standards Compliance
- **Regular Audits**: Periodic assessment of GDF adherence
- **Practice Reviews**: Regional practice compliance evaluation
- **Continuous Monitoring**: Ongoing tracking through Panorama dashboard
- **Improvement Planning**: Action plans for compliance gaps

### Governance Structure
- **Global Delivery Steering Committee**: Oversight and strategic direction
- **Practice Leadership**: Regional implementation and adaptation
- **Delivery Teams**: Day-to-day execution and feedback
- **D1D Team**: Framework development and continuous improvement

### Escalation and Support
- **Weekly Business Reviews (WBR)**: Regular performance assessment
- **Monthly Business Reviews (MBR)**: Comprehensive practice review
- **Quarterly Business Reviews (QBR)**: Strategic planning and goal tracking
- **1x2/2x2 Meetings**: Leadership alignment and decision-making

## Implementation Best Practices

### Team Enablement
- **Training Programs**: Comprehensive GDF training for all team members
- **Certification**: GDF competency validation and certification
- **Mentoring**: Experienced practitioners supporting new team members
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Regular sharing of best practices and lessons learned

### Tool Integration
- **Salesforce (SFDC)**: Primary system for opportunity and engagement tracking
- **Panorama**: Comprehensive reporting and analytics platform
- **PDP Catalog**: Searchable repository for delivery best practices
- **Regional Tools**: Practice-specific tools and systems integration

### Continuous Improvement
- **Feedback Collection**: Regular feedback from delivery teams and customers
- **Process Refinement**: Continuous improvement based on field experience
- **Best Practice Sharing**: Cross-practice knowledge sharing and adoption
- **Innovation**: Encourage innovation while maintaining consistency

This Global Delivery Framework ensures consistent, high-quality delivery across all AWS Professional Services engagements while maintaining the flexibility needed to meet diverse customer requirements and regional considerations.
